## 1. Meaning: "Tan" and Skin Color
When used to describe skin, **tan** can be both a noun, an adjective, and a verb:
 * **As an adjective/noun:** It describes a light brown or golden-brown color that skin develops after being exposed to the sun. (e.g., *"She has a beautiful tan,"* or *"His skin looks tan."*)
 * **As a verb:** It is the process of skin darkening from ultraviolet (UV) radiation. (e.g., *"I tan easily,"* or *"They went to the beach to tan."*)
Historically, the word comes from *tannery* processes, where animal hides were turned into leather using crushed tree bark (tannin), which turned the hides a distinct golden-brown color.

### Why your phone gets confused:
 1. **The "Pre-Nasal" Effect:** In English, when a vowel comes right before the letter **N** or **M** (like in ta**n** or te**n**), our noses naturally start to open up early to make the "N" sound. This distorts the vowel, making the short 'A' and short 'E' sound almost identical to an AI listener.
 2. **Regional Accents:** If you live in certain areas (like parts of the American South or Midwest), a linguistic phenomenon called the *Pin-Pen merger* or *Ae-raising* happens. This causes words like "tan" to naturally sound exactly like "ten."
